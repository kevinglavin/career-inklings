How to read your results

Understanding your results

John Holland's model sorts people and jobs into six interest types. People tend to be happiest when their work matches their type. This exercise reflects the interests you reported about yourself, so use it as a place to start exploring, not a final answer.

The hexagon

The six types are arranged in a ring: R, I, A, S, E, C. Types next to each other are alike. Types across from each other are most different. Your top two, Artistic and Social, are neighbors, which is why they fit well together.

Please note

Inklings is an informal interest-sorting exercise based on Holland's RIASEC model. It is not an empirically validated assessment and does not give a clinical or diagnostic result. For a validated measure with normed scores, consider Holland's Self-Directed Search. We encourage you to talk through your results with a career practitioner, and to change your code if a different order fits you better.
